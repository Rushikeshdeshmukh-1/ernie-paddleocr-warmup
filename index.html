AI for Social Good: Expiry Tracking & Food Donation System

Introduction

Food wastage is a growing global challenge, especially in developing countries like India. Millions of tons of edible food are wasted every year due to expiry mismanagement or lack of proper tracking systems. Meanwhile, countless individuals face hunger daily, relying on NGOs and community programs for support.

A large portion of food loss occurs at the retail level—small grocery shops, supermarkets, bakeries, and general stores—primarily due to manual inventory tracking that is prone to errors.

Problem Statement

Local retailers struggle to monitor expiry dates of hundreds of products. Checking each item manually is extremely time-consuming. As a result, items often expire unnoticed and are discarded, causing financial loss and preventable waste.

Challenges faced today

  • No automated inventory expiry tracking
  • No centralized system for notifying NGOs about near-expiry items
  • Lack of technology adoption in small retail shops
  • Food waste despite high need

Proposed Solution: AI-Powered Expiry Tracking System

The proposed platform uses OCR (Optical Character Recognition) and AI to scan product labels and automatically extract expiry dates, store product data, and send alerts before items expire. Shopkeepers can identify near-expiry products and sell them at discount or donate to NGOs.

How It Works

  1. Shopkeeper uses a mobile app to scan product barcodes or expiry labels
  2. PaddleOCR extracts product name, expiry date, batch number, and quantity
  3. System stores the details in a cloud database and tracks expiry periods
  4. Based on threshold alerts, AI provides notifications:
    • Near-expiry items available for discount sale
    • Donation availability updates to partnered NGOs
  5. Dashboard reports show wastage reduction and donation impact

Technology Stack

  • PaddleOCR — scans expiry text and layout from packaging
  • ERNIE — generates insights, recommendations, and automated reports
  • Cloud Database — structured inventory storage and analytics
  • Mobile / Web App — interface for scanning, alerts, and dashboards

Social Impact

This system creates large-scale positive impact by:

  • Reducing food waste significantly
  • Supporting NGOs and feeding the needy
  • Empowering small businesses through automation
  • Increasing affordability via discount sales
  • Contributing to UN SDG-2: Zero Hunger & SDG-12: Responsible Consumption

Future Scope

  • Integration with government food redistribution networks
  • Smart bins with sensors for automatic expiry detection
  • Machine learning-based consumption prediction models
  • Multi-language and handwritten label recognition
